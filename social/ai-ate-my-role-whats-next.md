## LinkedIn

```
Last week I published "The Anatomy of an AI-Native Org."

The most common message I got back was some version of: okay, but what happens to my role specifically?

PMs asked first. Designers asked next. EMs asked, slightly defensively. Junior engineers asked quietly, the way you ask a question you're afraid of the answer to.

So I wrote the answer.

The principle: every role splits along the same line. Translation work — converting one input into one output, running a process, coordinating others — collapses into agents. Judgement work — deciding what "correct" means, holding context, owning outcomes — grows. Dramatically.

Two arcs run through what follows.

Roles that evolve (same role, sharper):
→ Product Manager
→ Senior / Staff / Principal Engineer
→ Project / Program Manager
→ Engineering Manager

Roles that transform (different role, old name):
→ Junior Engineer
→ Designer
→ QA
→ Customer Success

A few things that landed harder than I expected:

▸ Product Managers and Project Managers are different skills. They've shared a title for a decade. The AI shift makes that impossible to ignore.

▸ For Product Managers — whichever lane you love (vision, intuition, research, or customer), your real job is becoming brutally direct: make money. Own the business number, not the roadmap.

▸ The hardest one to write was Junior Engineers. The apprenticeship pipeline that produced senior engineers is breaking, and the industry hasn't built a new one yet.

▸ QA isn't getting "its moment." The role transforms — from downstream service into embedded quality engineering, at par with software engineering, not downstream of it.

The transition belongs to the people who started six months ago, not the people who start six months from now.

Full walkthrough: https://ajeygore.in/content/ai-ate-my-role-whats-next
```
